▸ Climate & hazard filters
Each menu takes all ~67,000 candidate properties shown on this map, ranks them by one specific dataset, then hides the worse-scoring ones:
• Any — show every property
• Moderate — keep the better-scoring 2 out of every 3 properties
• Low (also labeled Cleanest / Coolest / Least) — keep only the best-scoring 1 out of every 3
Each menu below names the exact dataset it uses.
Natural hazards (FEMA)
Heat wave
Data: FEMA's heat-wave risk score — how often a place gets long, dangerous hot spells. Low = only the third of the map's properties with the least heat-wave danger.
Wildfire
Data: FEMA's wildfire risk score — the chance a wildfire reaches a spot. Low = only the third of the map's properties least likely to burn.
Drought
Data: FEMA's drought risk score — how often long dry spells (that dry up wells and gardens) strike. Low = only the least-drought-prone third of the map's properties.
Overall
Data: FEMA's overall risk score — every natural-disaster danger (heat, fire, floods, storms…) combined. Low = only the all-around safest third of the map's properties.
Air quality (asthma)
PM2.5
Data: EPA's fine-particle (PM2.5) readings — tiny smoke and soot you breathe in (wildfires, traffic, furnaces), bad for asthma. Cleanest = only the freshest-air third of the map's properties.
Ozone
Data: EPA's ground-level ozone (smog) readings — haze that builds on hot, sunny days and makes breathing harder. Cleanest = only the least-smoggy third of the map's properties.
Future climate (≈2050)
Heat
Data: federal climate-model projections for ~2050 — how much hotter summers are expected to get. Coolest = only the third of the map's properties projected to stay most comfortable.
Extreme rain
Data: federal climate-model projections for ~2050 — how many heavy 1-inch-plus downpours (flash-flood rain) are expected per year. Least = only the third of the map's properties projected to get the fewest.
Rankings compare the candidate properties to each other (not to the whole country). Western MA is climatically similar throughout, so the air-quality & climate menus split the properties less sharply than the hazard ones.
